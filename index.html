About the Author

Words written from lived experience.

E. Wise is an author, speaker, and longtime advocate for emotional recovery. His work draws from more than a decade of quiet practice — guiding individuals through the patient, deliberate work of healing what most people never speak about.

His earlier writing and accompanying course reached readers in over twenty countries, offering a steady voice to people rebuilding after grief, heartbreak, and self-doubt.

"We do not rise by forgetting the fire. We rise because we learned how to carry it."

Rising from the Ashes is his most personal work to date — a companion for anyone ready to meet their pain honestly and begin again.

— E. Wise

From Chapter I

The Weight We Learn to Carry

We do not choose the first wound. It arrives unannounced — a harsh word in childhood, a leaving that was never explained, a hand that should have steadied us but instead pulled away. We were too young to name it, so we named other things instead: shy, too sensitive, difficult.

By the time we are old enough to understand what happened, the wound has traveled. It lives now in our doubt, in our apology for taking up space, in the quiet voice that says don't. The original injury is almost impossible to see. What remains is only the shape it carved into us.

This is the work ahead: to return to that shape with patience. Not to undo it — the shape is ours now, and cannot be undone — but to meet it, to name it, and to carry it with a softer hand.
